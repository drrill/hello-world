# Dream jobs: Comedians in Cars Getting Coffee

When I started my current job, one line from the onboarding session stuck with me and has never left: "Crap jobs are created by other people. Dream jobs you make yourself." It comes from [Hugh MacLeod at Gaping Void](https://www.gapingvoid.com/blog/2017/02/09/the-best-jobs-will-never-be-televised/), and I think about it a lot.

I didn't really get it until I rediscovered Jerry Seinfeld's [Comedians in Cars Getting Coffee](http://comediansincarsgettingcoffee.com/). Here's a guy who loves comedy, loves talking about comedy with other comedians, loves cars, and appreciates a good cup of coffee. He built a show around exactly those four things. The title is the pitch. It's hiding in plain sight.

What makes it work is that probably only a handful of people on the planet could credibly make that show. Seinfeld didn't sit down with a whiteboard and reverse-engineer what the market wanted. He took the things he actually cares about, the things he'd do on a Saturday for free, and put a camera on them. Paul Jarvis makes a similar point in [Company of One](https://ofdollarsanddata.com/a-company-of-one/): the most durable work lives close to what you'd do anyway.

So the question I keep coming back to, for myself and for anyone who'll listen: what would your show be called? If you had to build your career out of four things you already love, what would be on the list? Write them down. Look at them. Then look at your calendar for next week.

The gap between those two pictures is the work.
