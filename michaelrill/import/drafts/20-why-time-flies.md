# Why Time Flies

A remarkable interactive explanation of something we all start to notice as we get older: the years go by faster. Maximilian Kiener's [Why Time Flies](https://www.maximiliankiener.com/digitalprojects/time/) is the best version of this idea I've come across. Scroll through it slowly. It's short, and it will change how you think about the shape of your own calendar.

The underlying idea is that we perceive time relative to how much of it we've already lived. When you're five, a year is twenty percent of your whole life. When you're fifty, it's two percent. The ratio keeps shrinking, and the subjective experience follows.

It's not a depressing thought, or at least I don't take it that way. It's a useful one. If the years get shorter from here on in, the argument for filling them with the things that actually matter gets stronger, not weaker. Oliver Burkeman's [Four Thousand Weeks](https://www.oliverburkeman.com/books) is the long, thoughtful version of the same observation. Kiener's piece gets you there in ninety seconds.
