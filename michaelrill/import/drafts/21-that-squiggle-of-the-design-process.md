# That Squiggle of the Design Process

Damien Newman's [Squiggle of Design](https://revisionlab.wordpress.com/that-squiggle-of-the-design-process/) is the best drawing of creative work I've ever seen. A tangled knot on the left, resolving into a clean line on the right. No legend required. Anyone who has ever tried to do original work recognises the shape immediately.

The reason I keep coming back to it: the knotted bit isn't a bug in the process. The knotted bit is the process. Most of the time you spend doing creative work, you are wandering around inside the knot, feeling lost, convinced you've taken a wrong turn. What the squiggle tells you is that the lostness is where the work lives. The straight line at the end is the small reward for surviving it.

I show this diagram to new team members more often than I probably should. Partly to set expectations. Partly to save them the anxiety of thinking the knot means they're failing. Tim Harford's [Messy](https://timharford.com/books/messy/) makes the longer version of the same argument, if you want a book on the topic. The squiggle is the two-second version.

Source: [That Squiggle of the Design Process by Damien Newman, via Revision Lab](https://revisionlab.wordpress.com/that-squiggle-of-the-design-process/).
