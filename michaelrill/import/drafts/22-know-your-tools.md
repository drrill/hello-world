# Know your tools

Seth Godin's short post [Dancing with tools](https://seths.blog/2020/05/dancing-with-tech/) is the best description I've read of a healthy relationship with technology. His point, roughly: the tool doesn't use you, and you don't fight it. You dance with it. You learn its strengths, you work around its limitations, and you stop expecting it to be something it isn't.

The thing that makes this advice useful rather than trite is that it applies equally to a hammer and to a spreadsheet and to an LLM. Every tool has a shape. The people who get the most out of a tool are the ones who take the time to learn that shape, rather than demanding the tool fit theirs. Matthew Crawford's [Shop Class as Soulcraft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shop_Class_as_Soulcraft) makes the long version of this argument with woodworking and motorcycle repair, and it lands in the same place.

Know your tools. Then dance with them.
