# The Digital Workplace Is Designed to Bring You Down

Cal Newport in a [New York Times magazine interview](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2023/01/23/magazine/cal-newport-interview.html) on how to actually do knowledge work in the current environment:

> So how do you actually work with your mind and create things of value? What I've identified is three principles:
>
> - doing fewer things,
> - working at a natural pace, but
> - obsessing over quality.
>
> That trio of properties better hits the sweet spot of how we're actually wired and produces valuable meaningful work, but it's sustainable.

I've been quietly testing this against my own calendar for a while now, and the hardest of the three, by some distance, is the first one. "Doing fewer things" sounds like the easy part. It's not. It's the part that requires saying no to things that are, by themselves, perfectly reasonable. The other two are almost automatic once the first one is in place. The first one is the battle.

Cal's [Slow Productivity](https://www.calnewport.com/books/slow-productivity/) is the book-length version if this interview catches you. Worth the time.
