# Decoder guest host Hank Green makes Nilay Patel explain why websites have a future

Nilay Patel knocked it out of the park with a recent pair of Decoder episodes. In the first one, he handed his own microphone over to [Hank Green](https://www.theverge.com/24087834/hank-green-decoder-podcast-google-youtube-web-media-platforms-distribution-future), who spent the hour interviewing Nilay on the future of the open web. Role reversals like this rarely work. This one does, because Hank is a genuinely great interviewer and because Nilay, freed from hosting duties, gets to be the guy with the full argument in his head rather than the guy keeping the conversation moving.

The thesis, roughly: platforms are good at distribution and terrible at being homes. Websites are the opposite. If you care about the work lasting longer than the platform of the month, you want your work living at a URL you control. Ben Thompson has written [a version of this argument](https://stratechery.com/2015/aggregation-theory/) from the business side, and Cory Doctorow's [enshittification](https://pluralistic.net/2023/01/21/potemkin-ai/#hey-guys) essays are the more acidic version. The Decoder episode is the friendly version, and it's the one I'd hand to a civilian.

I listened to it twice. The second time was better than the first.
